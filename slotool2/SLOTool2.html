Summary

Welcome to the IEA Student Learning Objective (SLO) Interactive Template. The purpose of the IEA SLO Interactive Template is to help you, as an educator, organize the ideas you will need to meet the requirements of the student growth portion of your Illinois Performance Evaluation. The SLO Interactive Template project was funded through a grant from the National Education Association (NEA). The IEA SLO Interactive Template is the result of a cooperative process involving active practitioners (50 Illinois National Board Certified Teachers and award winning IEA members from across the State) and Illinois Stakeholders including the Illinois State Board of Education, Center for Assessment Education, Analytics and others who work in the area of student growth assessment.

The IEA SLO Interactive Template does not replace meeting with your evaluator to determine the elements you will use to measure student growth, but it does provide a step-by-step process for organizing those elements and saving them both as an electronic file and a paper copy.

The IEA SLO Interactive Template does not provide choices for assessments. Your district PERA joint committee must provide criteria and guidelines, or must make decisions on which assessments you will use and/or develop. Please contact your district PERA joint committee for further guidance.

While the Illinois Education Association is available to answer questions and provide support for SLO development, SLOs require guidance by the district PERA joint committee. IEA encourages teachers to use existing resources, and channel questions through their district PERA joint committee. In the event that a question cannot be answered locally, please feel free to reach out to us.

Technical Information

Preferred Browser. The SLO Interactive Template works best in the Chrome browser – whether you are using a PC or a Mac. You can download Chrome for free by going to the following link, https://www.google.com/chrome/browser/desktop/

Font Size. The font style and size used throughout the template is Times New Roman 14.5. If you find the text size too small, you can use your browser to increase the display (Zoom). Please see the instructions below for four major browser programs:

Chrome-Click on the Chrome menu on the browser toolbar (upper, right side of your screen), and locate the “Zoom” section (roughly the middle of the popup menu). Clickto make everything on the page larger and click to make everything on the page smaller.
Firefox-Click on the Firefox menu on the browser toolbar (upper, right side of your screen), and at the top of the popup menu click to make everything on the page larger and click to make everything on the page smaller.
Internet Explorer-Click on the Internet Explorer Tools menu on the browser toolbar (upper, right side of your screen) and select the “Zoom” option (near the top of the popup menu). Choosing “Zoom in” or “Zoom out” increases or decrease your display by 25%.
Safari-Click on the Safari menu on the browser toolbar (upper, right side of your screen), and from the popup menu select “Customize Toolbar…”. This will open another popup box, and look for the “Zoom” icon. Left click and hold on the “Zoom” icon and drag it to a desired place on the Safari toolbar – a bit to the left of the icon would be a good spot. Now click on the large A to “Zoom in” or the smaller A to “Zoom out”.

iPad Support. While future iterations of the SLO Interactive Template will support iPads, this initial version of the Template is not designed for use with an iPad. You are strongly encouraged to use either a laptop or desktop computer.

Key Features:
Drop-down menus, Textboxes and Hover text.

Drop-down menus. Throughout the template is an opportunity for you to select responses. These are only suggestions. You may also, for any of the elements, type in a response if the drop-down list does not offer the response you need.

Select the best option. If the option does not appear, type it in.

Textboxes.You may type information into the textbox or copy and paste text from any existing file.Type information in the text box.

To adjust the size of the textbox, click, hold and drag the small, shaded area in the lower right corner.

Hover text. Simply move your cursor over any blue or red text and either a brief definition or links to additional resources will appear. Blue text also serves as links to additional resources. Click on the link and an additional resources page (relevant to the section of the tool on which you are currently working) will appear in a new tab.

Help and Support. The template provides help on the elements for the student learning objective process. Simply roll the cursor over any of the highlighted text in the template and you will find definitions and links to websites offering help in understanding and using the template. The training hyperlinks provide more information on the elements and will aid you as you complete the SLO Interactive Template.

Saving Content. If you need to leave the template before completing your work you MUST save your work to your computer. To save your work and export the file, click on the “Export” button found at the bottom of each page. When prompted, save the Text file on your computer in a place you can remember. The Export function will not work on Internet Explorer 9. When you re-enter the template, you will need to “Import” the data that you saved from your previous session. To do this, go to the Welcome tab, click on “Choose File” button and select your Text file from its location on your computer. The fields that you previously entered information will automatically populate. Please note that the Text file you export to your computer to save your work and then import back into the SLO Template to continue your work is a machine language file. It is only meant to communicate with the Template. Please do not alter the contents of this text file as you may end up losing information.

We also recommend reviewing the summary document with your evaluator for input, feedback or approval. After the evaluator approves the student growth measurement plan, both of you should sign and date the document. The plan includes a progress monitoring schedule. Please keep the document as a reminder of the timeline and a record of your student growth performance evaluation agreement.

If you have questions or comments about the template, please contact us at IEA Connect at ieaconnect@ieanea.org. To call IEA Connect: 888-IEA-1800 We appreciate your feedback.
